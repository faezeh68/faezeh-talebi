Introduction

Monkeypox is a viral infectious disease. It is a zoonotic infection that spread from animals to humans.

Symptoms of monkeypox can be in the range of mild to severe. Symptoms are included in skin lesions -like papule, pustule, and blister- fever, muscle pain, fatigue, and swollen lymph nodes.

Skin lesions can appear in different body areas like the face, palms, feet, throat, genital and anal areas.

After two or three weeks, symptoms will disappear. The infection can spread from human to human until skin lesions disappear from the skin.

In May 2022 some cases are reported in non-endemic countries and then the infection quickly spread to other non-endemic countries.

Since data is extracted from various sources, there are many gaps and overlaps in age groups, so I replaced age groups with their average. to do this, I split two numbers at both ends of the age spectrum in each age group and mutated the “age_left” and “age_right” columns, then I calculated the average of both columns and put the results in the “age_new” columns:

table(gsub(pattern = "-..", replacement = "", x = monkeypox_confirmed$Age))
table(gsub(pattern = "..-", replacement = "", x = monkeypox_confirmed$Age))

monkeypox_confirmed <- monkeypox_confirmed %>% 
  mutate(age_left = as.numeric(gsub(replacement = "", x = Age, pattern = "-..")),
         age_right = as.numeric(gsub(replacement = "", x = Age, pattern = "..-")),
         age_new = (age_left + age_right) / 2)

Conclusion

  1. There are 15734 confirmed cases from 74 countries between 2022/1/31 – 2022/7/21.

  2. The top 3 countries with the highest number of confirmed cases are Spain, the United States, and Germany.

  3. The highest number of hospitalizations belongs in Germany.

  4. The most common registered symptom is “skin lesions”, including papule, pustule, blister, rash, and ulcerative lesions with 31% of total symptoms. “Genital lesions”, “fever”, and “oral lesions” are placed in the next positions respectively.

  5. Less common registered symptoms like perianal lesions, adenopathy, cough, body/muscle pain, fatigue, headache, and throat are responsible for %8 of disease presentations.

  6. Most of the patients are in the average age of 45 (40-50 years).
